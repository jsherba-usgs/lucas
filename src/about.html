Our Team

The LUCAS model is developed by the Land use and Climate Change Team (LUCC) at the USGS Western Geographic Science Center (WGSC). LUCAS has been applied to model land-use and impacts on carbon storage and flux over a range of spatial and temporal scales. The model has also been used to track land-use attributes such as water demand and impacts on protected areas. Visit our team website to learn more about the model or explore the various model applications or contact us with questions.

The LUCAS App

The LUCAS application was developed in collaboration with the Geospatial Innovation Facility (GIF) at University of California, Berkeley. The web application is designed to ingest tabular and spatial results from LUCAS models and provide data visualization and download tools. The LUCAS model is built within the SyncroSim Framework, a framework for developing State-and-Transition Simulation Models developed by ApexRMS. Support for the development of the application came from the USGS LandCarbon program as well as the USGS Community for Data Integration (CDI)
